My PRK Experience: Day Fourteen

Thursday, April 24th 2015

I was able to go back to work this past week but it was hard to see my computer screen so I had to wear my bootleg eyeglasses which everyone thought was hilarious (ha.ha.). The vision in my right eye has been fluctuating drastically throughout the day/week. When I first wake up, I feel like I can see crystal clear out of my right eye, but as soon as I get to work, everything is blurry again. I had another meeting with my doctor this past Thursday and he said my eye is healing very slowly and he wants to see me in a week to determine when I can get the other eye done/make sure it's healed a bit more. I have feeling I'm going to be seeing him once a week for the next few weeks until my eye is healed before he decides that I can finally get my left eye done. Hopefully I'm wrong and he decides next week but we'll see. He also said that the fact that my eyes have been fluctuating so much means they're stil healing. I haven't been in any pain so that's been great. My eye has been very dry so I've been putting non-preservative lubricating drops in it very frequently. I'm going to start wearing a contact in my left eye because the eyeglasses with a patch is not a good look, hopefully I'm able to last throughout the day with my contact.

My PRK Experience: Surgery

Friday, April 10th 2015

So the surgery was pretty terrifying but all went well. I was so nervous (even though they gave me a pill to calm be down) and the fact that you're awake and can't close your eye made it a thousand times worse. I felt a lot of pressure from the doctor's tools and every time I saw the forceps, it made me queasy. They first put in numbing drops, then put in these drops that essentially ate away at my eye, then he started scraping all of the gunk off of my eye. Then they put a contact bandage over my pupil to help it heal. Thank goodness the surgery was only a few minutes, I think I would've thrown up if it was any longer. After the surgery, I just wanted to lay down. My eye felt exhausted and when I got home, I was in a lot of pain. My eye was throbbing and I wasn't given any pain medications or comfort drops so I took two Advils which seemed to do the trick. The pain went away within the hour and all was well. They taped a clear plastic guard over my eye which I took off later that night to make sure I could see. It was a little blurry but I could see so that was a relief! It was hard to open my eye and it was really sensitive so I covered it back up and went to sleep. The next day, I had to see the doctor bright and early for my follow up.



My PRK Experience: Pre-Operation

Thursday, April 9th 2015

Tomorrow, I'm having my PRK surgery done on my right eye after work. I'm pretty nervous/excited/anxious/scared + a million other emotions all rolled into one. I hope there's no pain and that I heal quickly so I can get my left eye done asap. A few of my friends and co-workers were telling me about their experiences and they made it seem like a piece of cake, but they all had LASIK which is slightly different that PRK. My healing time is much slower since the entire flap has to grow back, unlike LASIK where they lift, operate, and put the flap back down. I started putting antibiotic drops in my eye yesterday as per the doctor, and tomorrow they'll recheck my eyes and give me a Xanax before the surgery. I hope there are no complications, wish me luck!
